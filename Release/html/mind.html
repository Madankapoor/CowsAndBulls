Definitions of mind
v. - The intellectual or rational faculty in man; the understanding; the intellect; the power that conceives, judges, or reasons; also, the entire spiritual nature; the soul; -- often in distinction from the body. 2
v. - The state, at any given time, of the faculties of thinking, willing, choosing, and the like; psychical activity or state; as: (a) Opinion; judgment; belief. 2
v. - Choice; inclination; liking; intent; will. 2
v. - Courage; spirit. 2
v. - Memory; remembrance; recollection; as, to have or keep in mind, to call to mind, to put in mind, etc. 2
n. - To fix the mind or thoughts on; to regard with attention; to treat as of consequence; to consider; to heed; to mark; to note. 2
n. - To occupy one's self with; to employ one's self about; to attend to; as, to mind one's business. 2
n. - To obey; as, to mind parents; the dog minds his master. 2
n. - To have in mind; to purpose. 2
n. - To put in mind; to remind. 2
v. i. - To give attention or heed; to obey; as, the dog minds well. 2
