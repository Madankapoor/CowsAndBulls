Definitions of bend
v. t. - To strain or move out of a straight line; to crook by straining; to make crooked; to curve; to make ready for use by drawing into a curve; as, to bend a bow; to bend the knee. 2
v. t. - To turn toward some certain point; to direct; to incline. 2
v. t. - To apply closely or with interest; to direct. 2
v. t. - To cause to yield; to render submissive; to subdue. 2
v. t. - To fasten, as one rope to another, or as a sail to its yard or stay; or as a cable to the ring of an anchor. 2
v. i. - To be moved or strained out of a straight line; to crook or be curving; to bow. 2
v. i. - To jut over; to overhang. 2
v. i. - To be inclined; to be directed. 2
v. i. - To bow in prayer, or in token of submission. 2
n. - A turn or deflection from a straight line or from the proper direction or normal position; a curve; a crook; as, a slight bend of the body; a bend in a road. 2
n. - Turn; purpose; inclination; ends. 2
n. - A knot by which one rope is fastened to another or to an anchor, spar, or post. 2
