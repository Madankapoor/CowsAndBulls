lisp

Definitions of lisp
v. i. - To pronounce the sibilant letter s imperfectly; to give s and z the sound of th; -- a defect common among children. 2
v. i. - To speak with imperfect articulation; to mispronounce, as a child learning to talk. 2
v. i. - To speak hesitatingly with a low voice, as if afraid. 2
v. t. - To pronounce with a lisp. 2
v. t. - To utter with imperfect articulation; to express with words pronounced imperfectly or indistinctly, as a child speaks; hence, to express by the use of simple, childlike language. 2
v. t. - To speak with reserve or concealment; to utter timidly or confidentially; as, to lisp treason. 2
n. - The habit or act of lisping. See Lisp, v. i., 1. 2
