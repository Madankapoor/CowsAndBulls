Definitions of cram
v. t. - To press, force, or drive, particularly in filling, or in thrusting one thing into another; to stuff; to crowd; to fill to superfluity; as, to cram anything into a basket; to cram a room with people. 2
v. t. - To fill with food to satiety; to stuff. 2
v. t. - To put hastily through an extensive course of memorizing or study, as in preparation for an examination; as, a pupil is crammed by his tutor. 2
v. i. - To eat greedily, and to satiety; to stuff. 2
v. i. - To make crude preparation for a special occasion, as an examination, by a hasty and extensive course of memorizing or study. 2
n. - The act of cramming. 2
n. - Information hastily memorized; as, a cram from an examination. 2
n. - A warp having more than two threads passing through each dent or split of the reed. 2
