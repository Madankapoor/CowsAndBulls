Definitions of give
n. - To bestow without receiving a return; to confer without compensation; to impart, as a possession; to grant, as authority or permission; to yield up or allow. 2
n. - To yield possesion of; to deliver over, as property, in exchange for something; to pay; as, we give the value of what we buy. 2
n. - To yield; to furnish; to produce; to emit; as, flint and steel give sparks. 2
n. - To communicate or announce, as advice, tidings, etc.; to pronounce; to render or utter, as an opinion, a judgment, a sentence, a shout, etc. 2
n. - To grant power or license to; to permit; to allow; to license; to commission. 2
n. - To exhibit as a product or result; to produce; to show; as, the number of men, divided by the number of ships, gives four hundred to each ship. 2
n. - To devote; to apply; used reflexively, to devote or apply one's self; as, the soldiers give themselves to plunder; also in this sense used very frequently in the past participle; as, the people are given to luxury and pleasure; the youth is given to study. 2
n. - To set forth as a known quantity or a known relation, or as a premise from which to reason; -- used principally in the passive form given. 2
n. - To allow or admit by way of supposition. 2
n. - To attribute; to assign; to adjudge. 2
n. - To excite or cause to exist, as a sensation; as, to give offense; to give pleasure or pain. 2
n. - To pledge; as, to give one's word. 2
n. - To cause; to make; -- with the infinitive; as, to give one to understand, to know, etc. 2
v. i. - To give a gift or gifts. 2
v. i. - To yield to force or pressure; to relax; to become less rigid; as, the earth gives under the feet. 2
v. i. - To become soft or moist. 2
v. i. - To move; to recede. 2
v. i. - To shed tears; to weep. 2
v. i. - To have a misgiving. 2
v. i. - To open; to lead. 2
v. t. - To afford a view of; as, his window gave the park. 2
