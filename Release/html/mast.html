Definitions of mast
n. - The fruit of the oak and beech, or other forest trees; nuts; acorns. 2
n. - A pole, or long, strong, round piece of timber, or spar, set upright in a boat or vessel, to sustain the sails, yards, rigging, etc. A mast may also consist of several pieces of timber united by iron bands, or of a hollow pillar of iron or steel. 2
n. - The vertical post of a derrick or crane. 2
v. t. - To furnish with a mast or masts; to put the masts of in position; as, to mast a ship. 2
n. - A spar or strut to which tie wires or guys are attached for stiffening purposes. 2
