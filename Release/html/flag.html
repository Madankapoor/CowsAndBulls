Definitions of flag
v. i. - To hang loose without stiffness; to bend down, as flexible bodies; to be loose, yielding, limp. 2
v. i. - To droop; to grow spiritless; to lose vigor; to languish; as, the spirits flag; the streugth flags. 2
v. t. - To let droop; to suffer to fall, or let fall, into feebleness; as, to flag the wings. 2
v. t. - To enervate; to exhaust the vigor or elasticity of. 2
n. - That which flags or hangs down loosely. 2
n. - A cloth usually bearing a device or devices and used to indicate nationality, party, etc., or to give or ask information; -- commonly attached to a staff to be waved by the wind; a standard; a banner; an ensign; the colors; as, the national flag; a military or a naval flag. 2
n. - A group of feathers on the lower part of the legs of certain hawks, owls, etc. 2
n. - A group of elongated wing feathers in certain hawks. 2
n. - The bushy tail of a dog, as of a setter. 2
v. t. - To signal to with a flag; as, to flag a train. 2
v. t. - To convey, as a message, by means of flag signals; as, to flag an order to troops or vessels at a distance. 2
n. - An aquatic plant, with long, ensiform leaves, belonging to either of the genera Iris and Acorus. 2
v. t. - To furnish or deck out with flags. 2
n. - A flat stone used for paving. 2
n. - Any hard, evenly stratified sandstone, which splits into layers suitable for flagstones. 2
v. t. - To lay with flags of flat stones. 2
n. - One of the wing feathers next the body of a bird; -- called also flag feather. 2
