Definitions of bite

v. t. - To puncture, abrade, or sting with an organ (of some insects) used in taking food. 2
v. t. - To cause sharp pain, or smarting, to; to hurt or injure, in a literal or a figurative sense; as, pepper bites the mouth. 2
v. t. - To cheat; to trick; to take in. 2
v. t. - To take hold of; to hold fast; to adhere to; as, the anchor bites the ground. 2
v. i. - To seize something forcibly with the teeth; to wound with the teeth; to have the habit of so doing; as, does the dog bite? 2
v. i. - To cause a smarting sensation; to have a property which causes such a sensation; to be pungent; as, it bites like pepper or mustard. 2
v. i. - To cause sharp pain; to produce anguish; to hurt or injure; to have the property of so doing. 2
v. i. - To take a bait into the mouth, as a fish does; hence, to take a tempting offer. 2
v. i. - To take or keep a firm hold; as, the anchor bites. 2

v. - The wound made by biting; as, the pain of a dog's or snake's bite; the bite of a mosquito. 2
