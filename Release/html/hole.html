Definitions of hole
a. - Whole. 2
n. - A hollow place or cavity; an excavation; a pit; an opening in or through a solid body, a fabric, etc.; a perforation; a rent; a fissure. 2
n. - An excavation in the ground, made by an animal to live in, or a natural cavity inhabited by an animal; hence, a low, narrow, or dark lodging or place; a mean habitation. 2
n. - To cut, dig, or bore a hole or holes in; as, to hole a post for the insertion of rails or bars. 2
n. - To drive into a hole, as an animal, or a billiard ball. 2
v. i. - To go or get into a hole. 2
n. - A small cavity used in some games, usually one into which a marble or ball is to be played or driven; hence, a score made by playing a marble or ball into such a hole, as in golf. 2
n. - At Eton College, England, that part of the floor of the court between the step and the pepperbox. 2
