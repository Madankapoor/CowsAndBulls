Definitions of chop
v. t. - To cut by striking repeatedly with a sharp instrument; to cut into pieces; to mince; -- often with up. 2
v. t. - To sever or separate by one more blows of a sharp instrument; to divide; -- usually with off or down. 2
v. t. - To seize or devour greedily; -- with up. 2
v. i. - To make a quick strike, or repeated strokes, with an ax or other sharp instrument. 2
v. i. - To do something suddenly with an unexpected motion; to catch or attempt to seize. 2
v. i. - To interrupt; -- with in or out. 2
v. i. - To barter or truck. 2
v. i. - To exchange; substitute one thing for another. 2
v. i. - To purchase by way of truck. 2
v. i. - To vary or shift suddenly; as, the wind chops about. 2
v. i. - To wrangle; to altercate; to bandy words. 2
n. - A change; a vicissitude. 2

n. - A piece chopped off; a slice or small piece, especially of meat; as, a mutton chop. 2
