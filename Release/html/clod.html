Definitions of clod
n. - A lump or mass, especially of earth, turf, or clay. 2
n. - The ground; the earth; a spot of earth or turf. 2
n. - That which is earthy and of little relative value, as the body of man in comparison with the soul. 2
n. - A dull, gross, stupid fellow; a dolt 2
n. - A part of the shoulder of a beef creature, or of the neck piece near the shoulder. See Illust. of Beef. 2
v.i - To collect into clods, or into a thick mass; to coagulate; to clot; as, clodded gore. See Clot. 2
v. t. - To pelt with clods. 2
v. t. - To throw violently; to hurl. 2
