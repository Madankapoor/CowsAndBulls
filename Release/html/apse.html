Definitions of apse
n. - A projecting part of a building, esp. of a church, having in the plan a polygonal or semicircular termination, and, most often, projecting from the east end. In early churches the Eastern apse was occupied by seats for the bishop and clergy. 2
n. - The bishop's seat or throne, in ancient churches. 2
n. - A reliquary, or case in which the relics of saints were kept. 2
