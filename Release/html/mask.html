Definitions of mask
n. - A cover, or partial cover, for the face, used for disguise or protection; as, a dancer's mask; a fencer's mask; a ball player's mask. 2
n. - That which disguises; a pretext or subterfuge. 2
n. - A festive entertainment of dancing or other diversions, where all wear masks; a masquerade; hence, a revel; a frolic; a delusive show. 2
n. - A dramatic performance, formerly in vogue, in which the actors wore masks and represented mythical or allegorical characters. 2
n. - A grotesque head or face, used to adorn keystones and other prominent parts, to spout water in fountains, and the like; -- called also mascaron. 2
n. - In a permanent fortification, a redoubt which protects the caponiere. 2
n. - A screen for a battery. 2
n. - The lower lip of the larva of a dragon fly, modified so as to form a prehensile organ. 2
v. t. - To cover, as the face, by way of concealment or defense against injury; to conceal with a mask or visor. 2
v. t. - To disguise; to cover; to hide. 2
v. t. - To conceal; also, to intervene in the line of. 2
v. t. - To cover or keep in check; as, to mask a body of troops or a fortess by a superior force, while some hostile evolution is being carried out. 2
v. i. - To take part as a masker in a masquerade. 2
v. i. - To wear a mask; to be disguised in any way. 2
n. - A person wearing a mask; a masker. 2
n. - The head or face of a fox. 2
